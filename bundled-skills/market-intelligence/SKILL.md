# Market Intelligence

## When to Activate
- User mentions: competitor, benchmark, market, positioning, gap analysis, market research, competing channel
- Launching a new product or feature
- Quarterly strategy review
- Entering a new market or niche

## Two Modes

### Mode 1: Product Competitive Analysis
For SaaS/software products (QuantMind, future products).

**Framework:**
```
## Competitive Analysis: [Your Product] vs Market

### 1. Landscape Map
| Competitor | Target Market | Pricing | Key Differentiator | Weakness |
|-----------|--------------|---------|-------------------|----------|

### 2. Feature Comparison
| Feature | You | Comp A | Comp B | Comp C |
|---------|-----|--------|--------|--------|
| [Feature 1] | ✅/❌/🟡 | ... | ... | ... |

### 3. Positioning Gap
What can you do that they can't/won't?
- Technical moat: [algorithms, data, integrations]
- Market moat: [niche focus, geography, pricing]
- Distribution moat: [audience, partnerships, community]

### 4. Messaging Comparison
| Competitor | Headline | CTA | Tone |
|-----------|---------|-----|------|

### 5. Actionable Recommendations
1. [Exploit this gap]
2. [Avoid this fight]
3. [Differentiate on this axis]
```

## Zero-Bias Checks
- Am I only analyzing competitors I already know about? (Search for ones I might be missing)
- Am I over-weighting the market leader? (The #5 player might be the real threat)
- Am I comparing features when I should be comparing distribution?
- Am I copying what's popular instead of finding what's underserved?

## Guardrails
- NEVER copy competitors — analyze them to find GAPS, not to replicate
- ALWAYS include your own product/channel in the comparison (honest self-assessment)
- Focus on actionable gaps, not exhaustive comparison tables
- Update quarterly — markets shift fast
